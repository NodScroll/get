Nerdy Details

FILTER_ENGINE

EMA Smoothing

The app uses a low-pass filter (0.4/0.6 ratio) to eliminate webcam sensor noise and minor head jitters, ensuring the tracking HUD doesn't "shake."

GESTURE_LOCK

Spatio-Temporal Debounce

Nods are detected using a 0.5-second rolling window. To prevent "ghost scrolls," it enforces an 800ms cooldown lock after each gesture.

MOTION_LOGIC

Vertical Thresholding

A scroll only triggers when movement delta exceeds 4% of viewport height, filtering out normal facial micro-expressions.

VISION_CORE

Dual-Stage Processing

Face detection runs on a low-res 400px feed to maximize FPS, while the UI displays a high-res 660px preview.

Curious? (FAQ)

Is NodScroll safe? Does it record me?

NodScroll is 100% safe. Everything runs locally on your own computer. No images or video frames are ever captured, stored, or sent anywhere. No one sees your webcam — not even us.

Is it heavy on my CPU/Battery?

Nope! It's incredibly lightweight. The background dual-stage vision pipeline runs detection on a highly down-scaled feed just to find your head, ensuring maximum FPS while using minimal system resources.

Does it work with my glasses/headphones?

Yes. The tracking focuses on structural facial features and vertical movement, so your stylish glasses or bulky headphones won't confuse it at all.

What platforms are supported?
